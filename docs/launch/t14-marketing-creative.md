## Source Positioning

WillBuddy is the voice-first estate planning preparation tool for Texas families. It helps people talk through wills, guardianship, healthcare wishes, and powers of attorney, then turns those decisions into organized draft documents for attorney review.

Primary audience:

- Texas parents with young children who know they need to name guardians but keep postponing the conversation.
- Couples who want to prepare before paying an estate planning attorney.
- Solo planners who prefer a guided conversation over a long form.

Core message:

> Talk through the hard estate planning questions at home. Bring a clearer packet to your Texas attorney.

Required guardrails:

- Use "draft documents for attorney review" rather than "legal documents are complete."
- Use "Texas-focused" or "designed for Texas residents" rather than suggesting multi-state coverage.
- Keep "not legal advice" visible in long-form and partner copy.
- Do not imply WillBuddy replaces a lawyer, supervises execution, creates an attorney-client relationship, avoids probate, or guarantees legal validity.

## Claims-Risk Pass

This is an internal launch-copy risk pass, not legal advice or platform approval.

| Claim / phrase | Risk | Use? | Safer wording |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| "Texas-compliant documents" | Medium | Avoid in ads unless reviewed | "Texas-focused draft documents for attorney review" |
| "Legally valid will" | High | Do not use | "Draft will for attorney review and proper execution" |
| "Replace an attorney" | High | Do not use | "Prepare before meeting with an attorney" |
| "Save $1,000-$2,000" | Medium/high | Avoid as a direct ad claim | "Reduce discovery work before attorney review" |
| "$49 complete document package" | Medium | Use only if price is current | "$49 in current app pricing; verify before ad launch" |
| "Encrypted at rest and in transit" | Medium | Use only in product/FAQ where implementation is maintained | "Designed with privacy in mind" for ads |
| "Never used to train AI models" | Medium | Use only if provider/privacy language remains true | Keep in FAQ/privacy, not short ad copy |
| "Court decides without a guardian" | Medium | Use carefully | "Naming guardians helps document your wishes for review under Texas law" |
| "Attorney-ready" | Medium | Use with context | "Organized for attorney review" |
| Percentages/statistics from internal prompt context | Medium/high | Do not use in ads without a source review | Use qualitative phrasing such as "many families" |
| "Legally sound" | High | Do not use in ads | "Reviewed and finalized by a licensed Texas attorney" |
| "Texas families" | Low | Use | "Designed for Texas residents" |

Approved copy principles:

- Lead with emotional clarity and preparation.
- Name the document outputs as drafts.
- Pair legal-document claims with attorney-review language.
- Keep state scope explicit.
- Include "not legal advice" in long-form, partner, newsletter, and community copy.

Claims to block before launch:

- Any implication that WillBuddy creates final executable legal documents without attorney review.
- Any guarantee that documents will be accepted by a court, bank, healthcare provider, or probate process.
- Any ad implying emergency legal protection, guaranteed savings, or urgent fear-based outcomes.
- Any public post that targets users based on a personal tragedy, death, disability, or medical condition.
