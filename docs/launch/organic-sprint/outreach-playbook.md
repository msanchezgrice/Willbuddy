# WillBuddy partner outreach playbook

Prepared: 2026-07-13

## Goal

Earn relevant distribution, expert feedback, and editorial citations for genuinely useful Texas estate-planning resources. The objective is not to manufacture backlinks. A partner should be able to decline a link and still find the resource useful.

## Who to contact

Contact an organization only through a public editorial, programming, partnerships, newsroom, or general-inquiry route. Do not scrape staff emails, use member directories for prospecting, or send a pitch to a person merely because a professional directory exposes their details.

Good roles to look for on the organization’s own public site:

- newsletter or managing editor;
- community education or outreach lead;
- chapter/programming administrator;
- member education lead;
- caregiver-resource or parent-resource coordinator;
- newsroom tips desk;
- partnerships lead.

## What to offer

Lead with one useful item, not the entire company:

| Segment | First offer | Secondary offer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Estate-planning council or attorney group | Review the accuracy and limits of one tool | Co-review a public explainer or answer methodology questions |
| Financial planner or CPA chapter | Free member/client education resource | 20-minute virtual demonstration for programming staff |
| Realtor association | Readiness or intestacy resource for homeowner education | Newsletter-ready copy |
| Aging or caregiver organization | Power-of-attorney navigator | Plain-language handout with no sales CTA |
| Parent organization | Readiness quiz focused on guardians and decision-makers | Short parent-newsletter module |
| Wedding/newlywed publisher | “After the wedding” checklist | ToastBuddy/WillBuddy newlywed article |
| Local newsroom | Original Texas readiness data after publication gates | Interview, anonymized aggregate table, methodology, expert-review contact |

## Five-minute personalization check

Do not send until every box is true:

- [ ] I read the organization’s current programs, audience, or editorial page.
- [ ] I can name the exact audience need this one resource addresses.
- [ ] The contact route explicitly accepts this kind of inquiry or is a general organization route.
- [ ] The URL returns `200` and the result flow works on mobile.
- [ ] The message contains no legal-advice, validity, savings, or probate-avoidance claim.
- [ ] The message asks for feedback or optional sharing, not a ranking link.
- [ ] The UTM source identifies the organization, not an individual.
- [ ] The sender identity, physical postal address, opt-out instruction, and suppression list are ready.
- [ ] The prospect has not opted out and is not inside the 30-day cooldown.

## Outreach sequence

Use a low-volume, manually reviewed sequence. Suggested maximum: 8–10 new organizations on a send day.

| Day | Action |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Send one personalized message with one primary resource. |
| 4 | Send a short follow-up only if the first message was deliverable and no opt-out occurred. Add one useful detail, not “bumping this.” |
| 11 | Send a final close-the-loop note. Offer the copy block and state that no response is needed. |
| 12+ | Stop. Move to nurture only if the organization explicitly invites updates. |

## Templates

Replace every bracket. Delete any sentence that is not true for the recipient.

### 1. Resource-page or newsletter editor

**Subject:** Free Texas resource for [audience/program]

Hi [name or team],

I was looking at [specific page, newsletter, or program] and noticed you help [specific audience need]. We built a free Texas-focused [readiness quiz / document-role navigator / intestacy visualizer] that may be useful alongside that work.

It gives the educational result without requiring payment, an account, or an email address: [tracked URL]

WillBuddy is not a law firm, and the tool does not provide legal advice or validate anyone’s documents. If it is useful, I can send a 75-word newsletter blurb or a plain resource-card snippet. If it is not a fit, no response is needed.

Would you be open to taking a look?

[signature]

[postal address]

You can reply “no more outreach” and I will remove this address.

### 2. Professional council or expert-review request

**Subject:** Texas estate-planning tool: request for narrow expert feedback

Hi [council/programming team],

WillBuddy has released a free educational tool about [specific topic]. I am contacting the organization—not using its member directory—because your public mission includes [education/programming language from source].

The tool is here: [tracked URL]

The narrow request is whether the explanation and its limitations are clear enough for a Texas consumer preparing questions for a licensed attorney. We will not describe feedback as an endorsement, and we will not publish a reviewer’s name without written permission.

If formal review is outside your scope, would this be more appropriate as a future education-program idea or not a fit at all?

[signature and compliance footer]

### 3. Parent or caregiver organization

**Subject:** A no-email Texas checklist for [parents/caregivers]

Hi [team],

Your [specific resource/program] helps [audience] handle decisions that can otherwise feel overwhelming. We built a free Texas-focused [readiness quiz / power-of-attorney navigator] that breaks one adjacent planning question into a short guided flow.

[tracked URL]

It does not ask for payment or an email to show the result. It is educational, is not legal advice, and does not inspect or validate documents. If you think it could help your audience, I can provide a short neutral blurb for your resource page. There is no paid placement or reciprocal-link request.

Would it be useful for your team to review?

[signature and compliance footer]

### 4. Newlywed or wedding publication

**Subject:** An “after the wedding” resource for Texas couples

Hi [editor/team],

Your [specific planning guide/checklist] helps couples get through the wedding itself. We prepared a practical follow-on resource: a free Texas estate-planning readiness quiz for the decisions a new household may want to discuss after the celebration.

[tracked URL]

There is no email gate, and it is clearly framed as educational preparation rather than legal advice. We also have an original article draft, “After the Toast: Five Estate-Planning Conversations for Texas Newlyweds,” if a service-oriented guest contribution fits your editorial calendar.

Would the quiz or article be useful to your readers?

[signature and compliance footer]

### 5. Research-response partner

Use only after the deployed collection event, privacy copy, durable count, and export have been verified.

**Subject:** Help broaden a transparent Texas readiness sample

Hi [team],

WillBuddy is collecting five anonymous multiple-choice answers for an openly documented Texas estate-planning readiness project. The survey does not request names, emails, asset values, or document text, and the report will describe the sample as voluntary rather than representative.

[tracked research URL]

We will not publish Texas findings before 200 complete responses or publish a subgroup with fewer than 25 responses. Would you consider sharing the survey once with [specific audience]? We can provide the final aggregate table and methodology when it is published.

[signature and compliance footer]

### 6. First follow-up

**Subject:** Re: [original subject]

Hi [name or team],

One detail that may help with your review: [one genuinely useful fact, such as “the result appears before any product continuation” or “the navigator separates five document roles”].

Here is the resource again: [tracked URL]

[signature]

### 7. Final follow-up

**Subject:** Closing the loop: [resource]

Hi [name or team],

I will close the loop after this note. Here is a ready-to-use neutral description in case the resource is helpful later:

> [Use the relevant 35- or 75-word block from the partner kit.]

Resource: [tracked URL]

No response is needed. If you do not want future WillBuddy outreach, reply “no more outreach.”

[signature and compliance footer]

### 8. Positive-response reply

Thank you. Here are the items you requested:

- live resource: [URL];
- approved description: [copy];
- image: [public asset URL];
- methodology/limitations: [relevant section URL];
- contact for corrections: [address].

Please edit the copy for your audience. There is no requirement to link, and please do not imply that WillBuddy provides legal advice or that your organization endorses WillBuddy unless we have separately agreed to that language in writing.

## Outreach quality scoring

Score each draft before sending. Require at least 8/10 and no zero in “fit” or “truth.”

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Audience fit | Generic blast | Broadly relevant | Names a current program or reader need |
| Truth | Contains unsupported claim | Accurate but vague | Accurate, scoped, and limitations included |
| Ask | Link-first or ambiguous | Optional share | One specific review/share/programming request |
| Friction | Long and attachment-heavy | One URL | One URL plus copy in the message |
| Compliance | Missing route/footer/opt-out | Partially ready | Public route, accurate sender, postal footer, opt-out and suppression ready |

## Stop conditions

Stop outreach immediately when:

- the recipient opts out;
- the organization prohibits vendor or commercial submissions;
- a directory says its contact data cannot be used for solicitation;
- the tool URL is unavailable or its disclaimer is missing;
- the message would require a claim the current product cannot support;
- the sender cannot process opt-outs;
- the research privacy and count pipeline has not been verified.
