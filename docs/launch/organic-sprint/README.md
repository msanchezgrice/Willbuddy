# WillBuddy organic authority sprint

Prepared: 2026-07-13  
Operating window: 2026-07-13 through 2026-08-09  
Owner: WillBuddy

This folder turns the four-week organic recommendation into an execution queue. It contains copy and operating instructions, but it does not represent sent outreach, secured coverage, purchased media, or completed provider actions.

## Start here

1. Use [`four-week-operating-calendar.md`](./four-week-operating-calendar.md) as the daily runbook.
2. Use [`partner-kit.md`](./partner-kit.md) for approved claims, blurbs, newsletter copy, and resource links.
3. Use [`outreach-playbook.md`](./outreach-playbook.md) to personalize and quality-check each message.
4. Work down [`texas-partner-prospects.csv`](./texas-partner-prospects.csv) in priority order. Verify each public contact route immediately before outreach.
5. Hold [`texas-readiness-report-launch-packet.md`](./texas-readiness-report-launch-packet.md) until every publication gate is satisfied.
6. Use [`texas-search-pilot.md`](./texas-search-pilot.md) only after its WillBuddy account and conversion gates pass.

## Non-negotiable gates

- Do not call the Texas readiness survey representative. It is a voluntary convenience sample.
- Do not publish original Texas findings before 200 complete, auditable responses.
- Do not publish any subgroup with fewer than 25 responses.
- Do not imply that a tool gives legal advice, validates a document, replaces an attorney, creates a trust, or guarantees a legally valid result.
- Ask partners to share a useful resource because it serves their audience. Do not buy links, require links, automate bulk outreach, or arrange reciprocal links for ranking purposes.
- Do not use professional-member directories for unsolicited marketing. Contact the organization through its public programming, editorial, partnership, or general inquiry route.
- Do not send a commercial email until the sender identity, postal-address footer, opt-out path, and suppression process are ready.

## Measurement convention

Partner links use:

```text
?utm_source=<partner_slug>&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=texas_tools_202607&utm_content=<placement>
```

Earned-media links use:

```text
?utm_source=<outlet_slug>&utm_medium=earned&utm_campaign=texas_readiness_report_2026&utm_content=<pitch>
```

Track each placement independently. A reply, a live link, a survey completion, a tool completion, and a plan start are different outcomes.

## Source and compliance basis

- [Google people-first content guidance](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content) emphasizes original information, clear sourcing, demonstrable expertise, and useful content.
- [Google spam policies](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials/spam-policies) prohibit paid links, automated link creation, and excessive link exchanges intended to manipulate rankings.
- [FTC CAN-SPAM guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business) applies to commercial email, including business-to-business messages.
- [NAEPC](https://www.naepc.org/) and the [San Antonio Estate Planners Council](https://www.sanantonioepc.org/council/about) explicitly restrict unsolicited use of member directories. This sprint targets organization-level public routes instead.
