# WillBuddy Paid Ads Test Plan — July 2026

**Goal:** Drive qualified Texas parents to https://mywillbuddy.com/tools (free tools = low-friction entry), measure tool → signup → paid conversion.
**Budget:** ~$1,500/mo total. **Duration:** 4-week test.
**Positioning shift:** Existing ads = fear of having no plan. New ads = value-first (free tools), transparency (cost), and ease (15–20 min voice session). Curiosity and empowerment, not dread.

## 3. Creative concepts (3 angles × static + video)

### Concept A — "Free tools hook" (PRIMARY, ~50% of Meta spend)
Insight: Nobody wants to "buy a will" from an ad. Everyone will click a free 2-minute answer to a question they've quietly wondered about.

**Hook:** *"If you don't decide, Texas law does."* → intestacy visualizer.

**Meta primary text (v1):**
> Texas already has a plan for your family. It's just probably not your plan. Our free intestacy visualizer shows exactly who inherits what if you die without a will — spouse, kids, separate vs. community property, all of it. No signup, no email, source-linked to the Texas Estates Code. 2 minutes to an answer you've been putting off.

**Meta primary text (v2, shorter):**
> Who inherits your house if you don't have a will? In Texas, the answer surprises most parents. See yours in 2 minutes — free, no signup.

**Headlines:** "See who inherits — free tool" / "Texas law has a default. Check it." / "Free Texas intestacy visualizer"
**CTA button:** Learn More → https://mywillbuddy.com/tools/texas-intestacy-calculator
**Static image guideline:** Cream bg (#FAF8F5), phone mockup showing the flowchart, serif headline, sage (#5B7A5E) CTA pill, WillBuddy wordmark. No stock-photo families, no gavels, no gray "death" palettes. See §6 Higgsfield prompt A.

**Video (15s, 9:16 — Remotion build, see §7):** animated flowchart branches deciding "your" estate → tool cards → CTA. Motion-graphics, brand tokens, big captions (85% watch muted).

### Concept B — "Cost transparency" (~30% of Meta spend + main Google angle)
Insight: #1 blocker after procrastination is "an attorney costs $1,500+ and I don't know what I actually need."

**Hook:** *"What does a will actually cost in Texas?"* → cost calculator.

**Meta primary text:**
> Attorney will packages in Texas run $1,500+. Some people need that. Many new parents don't — but nobody tells you which one you are. Our free cost calculator lets you edit every assumption (no fake one-number promise) and see what preparation AND probate actually cost. Then decide with real numbers.

**Headlines:** "Run the numbers first — free" / "Will costs, without the sales call" / "Free TX estate cost calculator"
**CTA:** Learn More → /tools/texas-estate-planning-cost-calculator
**Static guideline:** split-panel comparison card ($1,500+ attorney vs. free calculator), editorial fintech look. §6 prompt B.

### Concept C — "Voice ease / 15 minutes" (~20% of Meta spend)
Insight: The product's real differentiator — talk it through out loud while holding a baby, instead of a 40-field form.

**Hook:** *"The will can ~~wait~~ takes 15 minutes."*

**Meta primary text:**
> You can't type a legal questionnaire one-handed at 2am. You can talk. WillBuddy walks you through guardianship, healthcare wishes, and powers of attorney by voice — 15–20 minutes, in plain English — then gives you organized Texas draft documents to bring to an attorney. Start with the free tools, no account needed.

**Headlines:** "Talk it through. 15 minutes." / "Estate planning, one-handed" / "Voice-first. Built for new parents."
**CTA:** Learn More → /tools (readiness checklist first)
**Static guideline:** photoreal parent + sleeping newborn, speaking toward phone, warm light. §6 prompt C.
**Video (Higgsfield):** UGC-feel lifestyle clip, captions overlaid. §6 prompt D.

**Compliance line for all creatives (small text or first comment):** "WillBuddy is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Documents are drafts for review by a licensed attorney. Texas residents."
Never claim: "legally binding will," "replaces a lawyer," "court-approved."

## 5. Measurement & test design

- **UTMs:** `?utm_source={fb|ig|google}&utm_medium={paid_social|cpc}&utm_campaign=tools_test_jul26&utm_content={a_intestacy|b_cost|c_voice}_{static|video}_{v1|v2}`
- **PostHog events to confirm before launch:** `tool_started`, `tool_completed` (per tool), `signup`, `checkout_completed`. Meta Pixel + Google tag mapped to the same.
- **Primary experiment KPI (July 15 iteration):** `ToolStart` and `ToolComplete`, segmented by destination and `utm_content`. Landing-page views are delivery diagnostics, not the success metric.
- **Optimization guardrail:** Keep the current LPV campaign as the control until `ToolStart` has enough observed volume for Meta to optimize reliably. If that threshold is reached, test a duplicate ad set within the same $30/day campaign ceiling; do not rewrite the control or increase spend.
- **Message-match decision:** Keep A and B on their matching direct tool routes. Pause C until a public voice-first route can deliver the advertised experience without an account wall; generic `/tools` is not a valid destination for that promise.
- **Success criteria @ 4 weeks:** CPC < $2.50 Meta / < $6 Google; tool completion rate > 35% of clicks; cost per signup < $40; any paid conversions = bonus at this budget.
- **Week 1–2:** all 3 concepts live, equal-ish spend. **Week 3:** kill bottom concept, move budget to winner + new variant of it. **Week 4:** landing page test (tools index vs. direct-to-tool).
- One variable at a time: concept first, then format (static vs video), then copy length.

## 8. Why these will out-test the fear ads

Fear creative converts the already-anxious but burns out fast and invites scroll-past guilt. Value-first creative gives a 2-minute win before asking anything, which (a) matches the /tools page promise exactly (message match → cheaper CPCs via better relevance/QS), (b) fills retargeting pools with people who already used a tool, and (c) lets the intestacy tool deliver the "oh no" moment organically — the tool does the fear work, the ad doesn't have to.
